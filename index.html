As recently elected Master of the Alpha Epsilon Pi fraternity at UVA, I inherited a very different fraternity than the one I pledged three years ago. With the graduation of the (re)Founding Fathers and the initiation of 16 new brothers, AEPi Mu has taken an entirely new form. Over the past three years I have been lucky enough to watch the fraternity develop both as a brotherhood and as a center for Jewish life at the university, and I'm both optimistic and confident about its continued development. This semester promises brotherhood events, tailgates, alumni and parents weekends, and (with luck and hard work) our most lucrative philanthropy events yet, and I could not be more excited to participate. The brothers are heavily involved in student life and ambitious about the ways by which we can even further establish the AEPi name in both the social scene and Jewish life here at the university. It's going to be a hell of a year.


Joe DuBois

President, AEPi Mu Chapter
